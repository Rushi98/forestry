Flowering
  • Bamboo is monocarpic – seeds once in lifetime
  • Herbaceous bamboo – flowers annually
  • Woody Bamboo – flowers infrequently
  • Due to less flowering, seed availability is less therefore reproduction is via vegetative propagation,
Types of flowering
Annual
  • Flowers every year
  • No death after flowering
  • Arundinaria weightiana
Periodic
  • Most cases
  • D. strictus – 30–70 year flowering interval
  • D. hamiltoni – 30–40 year interval
  • Die after flowering
Sporadic
  • Irregular flowering
  • few culms in a clump
  • D. hamiltoni
Gregarious
  • flowering proceeds from one end of forest to another like a wave
  • entire forest completes flowering cycle in 2–3 years
  • Dies after flowering

Why Bamboo die after flowering?

Not clearly understood – two thoughts

  1. Seed production requires immense energy → causes internal stress → death
  1. Parental bamboo dies by creating suitable environment for emerging seedling from seed → seedling face less competition

Natural regeneration
  • Occurs after gregarious flowering
  • Seedling emerges in abundance after rain
    • Only a few culms established into mature bamboo
    • It takes 5–10 years
  • Threats – grazing, fire
Artificial regeneration
Seed
  • Collect seed from forest after maturity
    • Shake culms
    • Spread cloth on ground
  • Direct sowing
    • Place seed 10–15 cm deep inside soil
    • Use 1 – 1.5 kg seed per ha
  • Nursery
    • Soak seed 24–48 hours for germination
    • Create 1-2 cm thick sand bed
    • Irrigation two times a day
    • Settling of 10 cm is put in to poly bag
    • Seedling of 30–45 cm can be transplanted to field
Vegetative propagation
Rhizome cutting
  • 10–15 cm rhizome cut from mature rhizome
  • Better than direct sowing
Rhizome planting
  • rhizome mass is divided into small division → directly planted in field
Offset
  • Culms are slant cut at 1–1.2 m above ground with rhizome and root intact
  • They are directly planted in field at 7.5 m x 7.5 m spacing
  • Used when there is no seed
Entire culm planting
  • One year old culm is removed without any damage
  • Placed in raised nursery bed with loose loamy soil of 2–3 cm thickness.
  • Shoot emerges in a month
  • Each rooted node with shoot is separated and planted in polybag
  • Seedling > 30cm height can be transplanted to field
Layering
  • Culm is partially cut at base
  • Culm is then bend down and buried into soil
  • Sprouting will arise
Micropropagation
  • Earlier explants are collected from seedlings
  • Now standard technique available for - B. bambos, D. hamiltoni, D. strictus
Establishment of bamboo plantation
  • Most cultivate - Bambosa bambos, D. strictus
  • Clear plantation site - remove weed, termite and insects
  • Spacing
    • Smaller species: 4x4 m
    • Larger species: 10 x 10 m
  • Dig pit for plantation
    • 30 x 30 cm for seedling and branch cutting
    • 60 x 60 cm for offset and rhizome planting
  • Time – before monsoon
  • Wedding – three times in first year, then two times
  • Soil loosening – two times a year
  • Protect from fire and grazing
  • Irrigation in initial years
Harvesting

Harvest only mature culms

  • Usually found at center

Select > 3 year old culms

  • Felling intensity 70%
  • Return return 30% old culms to protect emerging culms

  • Crop Character

    Uneven aged

  • Felling/cutting cycle and felling rules
    • felling cycle of 3–4 years
    • Rules
      • No cutting of 1–2 year-old culms
      • Retain some old bamboo to support immature culms
      • No digging of rhizomes
      • Cutting
        • Minimum height – 15 cm (retain minimum 1 node)
        • Sharp instrument so that the stump does not split
        • Flowering – cut only seed has been shed
